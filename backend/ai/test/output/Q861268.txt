Katarina-Sofia was an interesting borough in central Stockholm, named after the parishes of Katarina and Sofia. It was part of the vibrant Södermalm area and even included parts of Hammarby Sjöstad. This area had a population of about 42,750 as of 2004, showcasing a rich blend of history and culture.

However, it’s worth noting that in 2007, Katarina-Sofia merged with the borough of Maria-Gamla Stan, forming what we now know as Södermalm. If you're keen on exploring the historical roots of this area, there are plenty of stories to uncover about its development and cultural significance in Stockholm.
