You’ll definitely want to check out Konstnärshuset while you’re in Stockholm, Jon. This beautiful building, owned by the Swedish Artists Association, has been a cornerstone of the city's art scene since it opened in 1899. Designed by Ludvig Petersen, its architecture blends Italian and Spanish styles from the 16th century, which makes it quite a sight with its detailed façade and stunning mosaics.

Inside, you'll find a rich exhibition hall and a charming restaurant, Konstnärsbaren, restored in 1931. It’s adorned with exquisite wall paintings, adding to the ambiance of the place. Not to mention, the building is filled with works from celebrated artists and craftsmen, so it's a great spot to immerse yourself in Swedish art history.
