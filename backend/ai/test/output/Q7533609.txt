In 1867, the first official charter established a formalized education for naval cadets, which included rigorous training in subjects like navigation, naval warfare, and even languages like English and French. Over the years, the academy adapted, moving locations and expanding its programs, finally settling into a specially constructed building on Skeppsholmen.

The academy not only focused on theoretical education but also emphasized practical skills, with cadets often training on real ships. It became a beacon of military education, influencing many who went on to serve in the Swedish Navy. Today, the original building serves a different purpose, hosting an organization that addresses issues concerning Northern Europe and the Baltic region.
