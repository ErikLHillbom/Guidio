You know, if you’re interested in history and culture, Oscarsteatern is a must-see in Stockholm. It’s not just any theatre; it’s the best-known musical theatre in Sweden, located right at Kungsgatan 63. 

This beautiful venue opened back in 1906, designed in the stunning Art Nouveau style by Axel Anderberg. It was named after King Oscar II and originally had a whopping 1,175 seats, all adorned with intricate white stucco and gilded decorations. 

Over the years, Oscarsteatern has hosted some iconic productions, like *My Fair Lady* and *The Phantom of the Opera*. It truly has a rich legacy in the realm of musical theatre, making it a cultural landmark in the city. If you get a chance to catch a show there, Jon, it's definitely worth it!
