The Royal Swedish Opera, or Kungliga Operan, is a fascinating piece of Stockholm's cultural heritage, Jon. Nestled in the heart of the city, it's not just an opera house; it's steeped in history, dating back to its founding by King Gustav III in 1773. The first performance was held in 1773, and it was the first native-speaking opera in Sweden.

The current building, inaugurated in 1899, is a striking neo-classical structure with a grand gold foyer and elegant marble staircase. Did you know it also houses one of Europe’s oldest orchestras? The Royal Swedish Orchestra dates back to 1526, making it quite a historic ensemble!

If you wander around, you’ll find it situated near other significant landmarks, like the Royal Palace.
