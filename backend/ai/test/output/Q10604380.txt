You’re in for a treat with Nybroviken, Jon! This bay is not just a pretty spot in central Stockholm; it’s steeped in fascinating history. Originally known as Ladugårdslandsviken, it was much wider back in the 17th century and transformed dramatically over the years. 

The name Nybroviken comes from a historical bridge called Nybro that once connected the area to Nybrogatan. Today, it’s a bustling departure point for ferries heading to Djurgården and the beautiful Stockholm Archipelago, which adds a lively atmosphere to the area. 

What’s particularly intriguing is how the bay underwent extensive land filling from the late 19th century, drastically changing its appearance and usability. And if you're around, don’t miss Berzelii Park nearby—it's a lovely spot to soak in the history while enjoying the views.
