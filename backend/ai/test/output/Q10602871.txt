Norrlandsgatan is quite an interesting street in Norrmalm, Stockholm, stretching from Birger Jarlsgatan to Hamngatan. It's not just a thoroughfare; it's a spot steeped in history. One significant aspect you might find fascinating, Jon, is that the famous restaurant Fontainebleau used to be located here until it was tragically destroyed in a bombing on New Year's Eve in 1982. It's a reminder of the city's resilient spirit and turbulent past. If you're exploring the area, you'll definitely get a sense of its rich history and cultural significance!
