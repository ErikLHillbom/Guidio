You know, Jon, if you’re looking for a slice of history in Stockholm, the Grand Hôtel is a must-see. Founded in 1872 by a Frenchman named Jean-François Régis Cadier, it’s widely regarded as the best hotel in the city. What’s really fascinating is that every Nobel laureate since 1901 has stayed there — it’s like a tradition!

Located right next to the Nationalmuseum and just across from the Royal Palace and Gamla stan, the hotel has over 300 rooms and several swanky dining options, including a restaurant run by renowned chef Mathias Dahlgren. It’s not just a hotel; it’s part of the fabric of Swedish culture. From hosting significant events in history to celebrating milestones like women's suffrage, the Grand Hôtel has been at the center of it all.

So, if you have time, checking out this grand establishment would definitely enrich your experience of Stockholm’s history and culture!
