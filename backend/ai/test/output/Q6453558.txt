Have you had a chance to explore Kåkbrinken yet? It’s this fascinating old street in Gamla stan, running from the waterfront at Munkbroleden to the lively square of Stortorget. The name itself has quite an intriguing backstory, dating back to 1477, and it’s derived from an old Swedish term that relates to a pillory, referencing some dark historical events.

If you wander down Kåkbrinken, you’ll find a unique runestone at the corner with Prästgatan. This stone, likely from around AD 1000, was erected in memory of a family, showcasing the rich history of the area. It’s one of the oldest remnants you can see in Stockholm, and it’s even believed to have been crafted by a master carver and his apprentice.
