You know, Jon, if you're interested in history and culture, Birger Jarls Torn is definitely worth a look. This impressive defensive tower sits on Riddarholmen, which is part of Gamla Stan, the historic heart of Stockholm. 

Named after Birger Jarl, the legendary founder of the city, the tower was actually built in the 1530s under King Gustav I. It replaced older structures that had been destroyed by fire. Interestingly, some of the bricks used for its construction came from a nearby priory, giving it that historical edge.

Originally two stories tall, it has undergone several renovations over the centuries, including an expansion in the late 1500s and a major restoration in the mid-18th century, which added a fourth floor and a new roof. Nowadays, it houses the offices of the Chancellor of Justice, but parts of it are designed to make it more inviting to locals and tourists alike.
